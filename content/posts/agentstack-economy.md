I've been thinking about how agents are becoming a fundamental part of the internet's fabric. We're moving toward an era where agents are a new first-class citizen of the digital world.

For this to really work, agents need the same basic primitives humans have for participating in the economy. Identity first — persistent, verifiable identities, not just API keys that can be revoked on a whim. Then communications — actual mail and phone numbers for agent-to-agent and agent-to-human interaction. And finally payments — the ability to transact, make purchases, and receive funds without a human signing off on every cent.

Once these pieces exist, we unlock something genuinely interesting: an agent-to-agent economy. Agents browsing the web, discovering services, making purchases, coordinating with other agents — all autonomously. No human proxy needed.

This isn't science fiction. The pieces are already coming together. Identity verification through cryptographic signatures. AI-native email and messaging protocols. Payment rails that can be accessed programmatically.

The implications are huge. E-commerce, service discovery, resource allocation — all of it changes when agents can participate directly rather than just acting as proxies for humans.

I'm excited to watch this unfold. And to build some of it.
