AI has crossed a threshold. Claude 4.5 Opus and successors can now handle complex tasks end-to-end—coding, content, research, sales, marketing—without specialized models or constant hand-holding.

Platforms like OpenClaw demonstrate this: one agent, armed with skills, can execute a wide array of workflows. This is the infrastructure layer for autonomous companies.

Pioneers like Paperclip and Openfang are already building fully agentic organizations. These entities could manage their own social presence (LinkedIn, X), hire humans when needed, and even sell services peer-to-peer on platforms like Moltbook.

Two accelerants:

- **webMCP** — lets agents browse and transact as agents, not humans
- **x402 payments** — the payment rail that completes the loop

The fully autonomous company isn't science fiction. It's emerging now.
